# Article

Most Reuters graphics stories are built from the same handful of components, laced together inside the [`Article`](?path=/docs/components-page-layout-article--docs) text well. This composition strings them together in the order you'll usually reach for them — a working dress rehearsal for the real thing.

In a real project the whole story is wrapped in a [`Theme`](?path=/docs/components-styles-theme--docs) and bracketed by a [`SiteHeader`](?path=/docs/components-page-furniture-siteheader--docs) and [`SiteFooter`](?path=/docs/components-page-furniture-sitefooter--docs), which the graphics-kit page template provides for you.

## Putting it together

In the graphics kit the article body lives in `src/lib/App.svelte`. Rather than hand-placing each component, drive the body from a single structured `content` object — an ArchieML doc or `content.json` — and map over an ordered list of blocks. That keeps the markup small and the ordering data-driven, the same approach the [story source](https://github.com/reuters-graphics/graphics-components/blob/main/src/compositions/Article.stories.svelte) uses:

<Source
  language="svelte"
  dark
  code={`<Headline {...content} />

{#each content.blocks as block}
{#if block.type === 'text'}

<BodyText text={block.text} />
{:else if block.type === 'photo'}
<FeaturePhoto src={block.src} altText={block.altText} caption={block.caption} />
{:else if block.type === 'graphic'}
<GraphicBlock title={block.title} description={block.description} notes={block.notes} />
{:else if block.type === 'video'}
<Video src={block.src} ariaDescription={block.ariaDescription} notes={block.notes} />
{/if}
{/each}

<InfoBox title={content.infoBox.title} text={content.infoBox.text} />
<EndNotes notes={content.endNotes} />
<ShareBar url={content.shareUrl} headline={content.hed} />
<BioBox authors={content.bioAuthors} />
<ReferralBlock heading="Read next" stories={content.referrals} />
`} />

The whole `Article` is then wrapped in a `Theme` and placed between a `SiteHeader` and `SiteFooter` on the page — see [Page layout](?path=/docs/components-page-layout-intro--docs) for the surrounding structure.
